01 / Concept

The logo is the whole concept.

An isometric T inside a square grid — geometry that is itself a piece of engineering. The system extends this idea outward: a flat grid that becomes structural when projected into space. We don't decorate around the mark. We build out from it.

— 01 / Grid as foundation

Every artifact sits on the grid.

The 4×4 grid behind the logo extends to a 12-column layout grid across all surfaces. Margins scale, gutters are 16px multiples. The bones are visible in every artifact — partial grid lines fading into negative space, dimension callouts in mono type.

— 02 / Gradient as energy

Green to blue carries the spark.

The signature green-to-blue gradient is reserved for energy-carrying moments only — primary CTAs, key data points, accent words, gradient text on dark slides. Used 5–10% of any composition. Never as a background fill behind body content.

— 03 / Black does the work

Structural black, generous paper.

Black handles all heavy lifting — text, lines, dark surfaces. Paper holds 60% of every layout. The gradient never fights black for attention; it appears, makes its point, recedes. The result is quiet confidence with a single signature flourish.

02 / Logo system

One mark, measured applications.

The mark always sits on a clear field equal to one quarter of its own width. Six approved lockups handle every context — primary marketing surfaces, dark backgrounds, accent applications, and minimum-size scenarios where the mark stands alone.

05 / Voice & tone

Numbers are the brand's vocabulary.

Confident, specific, technical-but-readable. Lead with concrete capability — never with superlatives. If a sentence can be replaced with a number and a unit, replace it.

— Yes / On brand
"12 GW of generation capacity delivered across four continents since 1998."
Why: A specific number, a specific scale, a specific timeframe. The reader knows exactly what was done. No adjectives doing the work that nouns should do.
— No / Off brand
"We are an industry-leading provider of world-class energy solutions for tomorrow's needs."
Why: Five superlatives, zero facts. Every competitor's website says this. The sentence could be cut entirely and nothing would be lost.
— Yes / On brand
"Substation commissioned 14 days ahead of schedule. Outage window: 38 hours."
Why: Reads like an entry in a project log. Schedule and outage figures are what clients actually evaluate. The work speaks for itself.
— No / Off brand
"Our team's tireless dedication to excellence drove a successful project outcome."
Why: Pure connective tissue with nothing inside. "Tireless dedication" is a phrase, not a fact. Cut and replace with what was actually delivered.
